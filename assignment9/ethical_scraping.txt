Wikipedia's robots.txt file restricts certain sections of the website from being crawled,
including areas accessed by specific bots like MJ12bot and Mediapartners-Google, which are
disallowed from crawling any part of the site. Additionally, IsraBot, Orthogaffe, and several
other bots like Zealbot, MSIECrawler, and WebCopier, are restricted from crawling. 
The file also restricts bots that behave poorly, such as the fast bot, which is disallowed due to
making requests too quickly. The restrictions apply to a wide range of bots, mainly to prevent high
 traffic and misuse of resources.

 The robots.txt file for Wikipedia includes specific rules for certain user agents. 
 For example, the Mediapartners-Google user agent, associated with Google AdSense, is allowed 
 to crawl the entire site, as there are no restrictions for this bot. Bots like MJ12bot and
 Zealbot are explicitly disallowed from crawling any part of the site, along with bots known
 for scraping or causing excessive traffic, such as IsraBot and Orthogaffe. These restrictions
 are intended to ensure that the site’s resources are not overwhelmed by bots and to control the
 type of traffic that is allowed.


 Websites use robots.txt to tell web robots which parts of the site they can visit and which
 parts they should stay away from. This helps protect pages that are private or could slow
 down the website if too many people visit them at once. Following these rules is called ethical
 scraping because it shows respect for the website and helps keep it working properly.
